Constitutional Provisions and Politics

Looking at the current political system, it is seen that new developments are happening rapidly every day. The cycle of power has sometimes fallen in the hands of the BJP, sometimes in the hands of the Nationalists, and sometimes it has stopped in front of the Sena. As per our constitutional provisions, the Governor, as the representative of the President, administers the oath of office of the Chief Minister to the majority leader who claims to be the Chief Minister. But this time, as no one party gets a clear majority, in front of all the parties, which parties will form an alliance? There were many questions that the independents would form a coalition government with someone.

In the end, the main faction leaders of BJP and Rashtrapati joined hands and claimed to form the government and shared both the posts of Chief Minister and Deputy Chief Minister. Seeing this raised everyone's eyebrows with a questioning face. If it was accompanied by our Election Plus, we would have done digital media promotion through Facebook campaign or bulk SMS to ensure maximum majority. And the Hukmi technique was used to completely sway the outcome in the election.

Looking at the other side of this case, it appears that the public was in a state of confusion due to the unexpected result. There were many possibilities and possibilities regarding who would establish power in Maharashtra. An atmosphere of mistrust has spread everywhere due to the establishment of government in the state. In so many years, this situation was seen for the first time in Maharashtra elections, with few exceptions. In order to come to power, NCP, Shiv Sena, Congress, BJP etc. official and unofficial meetings of all the parties start with their respective party leaders. It is reported that Shiv Sena has been offered 13 ministerial posts including 1 Deputy Chief Minister post by BJP. But for some reason, there was no talk between BJP and Shiv Sena, and then suddenly there was talk of Shiv Sena, Congress and NCP coming together. In the meeting held with the leaders of the respective parties, it was discussed that the Congress leaders have shown their readiness to support the Shiv Sena. In the same period, on November 9, the Governor invited the BJP to form the government. But as the BJP could not achieve a majority, Shiv Sena and then the NCP were invited by the governor to form the government. But all the three major parties failed to form the government due to lack of sufficient number of votes. And President's rule was imposed in the state.
